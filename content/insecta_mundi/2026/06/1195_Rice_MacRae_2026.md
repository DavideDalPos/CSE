abstract: 'New ecological, distributional, behavioral, and host-association data are presented for 156 species and subspecies of longhorned beetles (Coleoptera: Cerambycidae) from Arizona, California, Connecticut, Florida, Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Michigan, Missouri, Oklahoma, Nebraska, New Mexico, North Carolina, and Texas. Detailed observations reveal extended developmental plasticity in <i>Eupogonius pauper</i> LeConte, variable pre-pupal behavior in <i>Lagocheirus araneiformis stroheckeri</i> Dillon, new girdling and oviposition hosts for <i>Oncideres cingulata cingulata</i> (Say) and <i>Oncideres rhodosticta</i> Bates, the importance of <i>Heracleum maximum</i> W. Bartram as an early-summer floral resource, and report northward range extensions in three cerambycid species — <i>Leptostylus cretatellus</i> Bates, <i>Lypsimena fuscata</i> Haldeman and <i>Polymitoleiopus sandersoni</i> (Gilmour) — potentially associated with climate change. <i>Atrypanius irrorellus</i> Bates is recorded for the first time from the United States. A review of BugGuide and iNaturalist records yielded four new state records for Missouri and 13 for Texas, underscoring the value of citizen-science data when coupled with expert verification. Predators of cerambycid larvae are newly documented from Cleridae: <i>Chariessa vestita</i> (Chevrolat), <i>Enoclerus quadrisignatus</i> (Say), <i>Monophylla pallipes</i> (Schaeffer), <i>Pelonides humeralis</i> (Horn); Trogossitidae: <i>Temnoscheila acuta</i> (LeConte); and Aves (Picidae).'
