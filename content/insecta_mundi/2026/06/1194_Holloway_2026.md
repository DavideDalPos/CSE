abstract: 'The Dermestidae (Coleoptera) of Jordan and Egypt are poorly known, and the current study represents the first extensive consideration of <i>Anthrenops</i> Reitter, 1881 (a subgenus of <i>Anthrenus</i> Geoffroy) in Jordan and Egypt. Three new species of <i>Anthrenops</i> are described: <i>Anthrenus</i> (<i>Anthrenops</i>) <i>pinnigeri</i>, from Jordan and Egypt, <i>Anthrenus</i> (<i>Anthrenops</i>) <i>carbonarius</i> from Jordan, and <i>Anthrenus</i> (<i>Anthrenops</i>) <i>rufus</i> from Egypt. Previously, only three species of <i>Anthrenops</i> had been definitively recorded from Jordan (<i>A. coloratus</i> Reitter, <i>A. eichleri</i> Kadej and Háva and <i>A. funiculus</i> Holloway) and one species from Egypt (<i>A. coloratus</i>). Therefore, comparisons between previously described species and the new species are extended to include <i>Anthrenops</i> species from Iran, a region that has been more thoroughly studied. Images of habitus, ventrites, antennae, and genitalia are presented. It is concluded that none of the previously described <i>Anthrenops</i> species resemble any of the new species described here. New records of <i>A. coloratus</i>, <i>A. eichleri</i> and <i>Dermestes intermedius</i> Kalík for Jordan are noted.'
