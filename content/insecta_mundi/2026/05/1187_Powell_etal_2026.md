abstract: 'An invasive armored scale, <i>Pseudaulacaspis rubra </i>(Maskell, 1898) (Hemiptera: Coccomorpha: Di¬aspididae), was detected in the Western Hemisphere for the frst time in January 2025. This species was found in abundance on mature mango trees (<i>Mangifera indica </i>L.) (Anacardiaceae) planted in the landscape in sub¬urban Miami-Dade County, Florida, USA. Habitus and slide-mounted images are provided. A natural enemy, the larva of a <i>Cybocephalus </i>sp. (Coleoptera: Cybocephalidae) beetle, was observed feeding on an adult female under the test. Herein we provide an updated species diagnosis for <I>P</I>. <i>rubra </i>with discussion on morphological variation and a key to slide-mounted adult females of four <i>Pseudaulacaspis </i>MacGillivray species present in Florida and two species of <i>Albastaspis </i>MacGillivray intercepted in Florida, with illustrations of each. We also provide host data for all Florida specimens of <i>Pseudaulacaspis </i>and <i>Albastaspis </i>in the Florida State Collection of Arthropods as well as novel sequence data for two species (<I>P</I>. <i>rubra </i>and <i>Albastaspis coloisuvae </i>(Williams and Watson)) treated in the study.'
