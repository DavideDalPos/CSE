abstract: 'We report the first documented rearing of <i>Brachys fasciferus</i> Schwarz (Coleoptera: Buprestidae), from a leaf mine collected on southern live oak (Fagaceae: <i>Quercus virginiana</i> Mill.) in North Carolina. This species was previously only known from adult specimens collected in the United States as far north as Georgia. Whereas all other Nearctic <i>Brachys</i> Dejean species that have been reared overwinter as larvae or pupae, we show that <i>B. fasciferus</i> overwinters as adults'
