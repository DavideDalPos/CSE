abstract: 'The avocado spanworm, a common foliar pest of avocados in Florida, has long been misidentified as <i>Epimecis detexta </i>(Walker), a species that may be restricted to Jamaica. Here, we describe <i>Epimecis guacamolearia </i>Matson, new species, to resolve this longstanding taxonomic misapplication. The new species is compared with <i>E. detexta </i>and <i>E. semicompleta </i>(Warren) as part of a proposed <i>detexta</i>-group within <i>Epime¬cis </i>Hübner. A lectotype is newly designated for <i>Bronchelia detexta </i>Walker. We also provide an overview of <i>Epimecis </i>taxonomy in the USA, including a summary of historical name misapplications, illustrations of adults and larvae of each species, and an examination of diet breadth across the genus using records from the long-term rearing program in Área de Conservación Guanacaste (ACG), Costa Rica, which yield new insights.'
