abstract: 'The discovery of specimens matching the original description and photographs of the type of <i>Enoclerus crabronarius </i>(Spinola) (Coleoptera: Cleridae) appears to resolve the identity of this heretofore problematic species. One of these specimens is illustrated here, and location data for this species are presented for the first time. <i>Enoclerus necator </i>new species is described from Zacapa, Guatemala and Chiapas, Mexico. <i>Enoclerus mocho </i>Rifkind is synonymized under <i>Enoclerus reburrus </i>Barr. New distributional records for Cleridae are presented: one new country record for Mexico; five new state records for Mexico; 19 new country records for Guatemala; one new department record for Guatemala; five new country records for Nicaragua; one new country record for Costa Rica; two new country records for Belize; three new country records for Panama; and one new province record for Panama.'
